## 1. "We've Achieved AGI" — Jensen Huang Just Changed the Conversation

[Verge coverage](https://www.theverge.com/ai-artificial-intelligence/899086/jensen-huang-nvidia-agi)

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang said it out loud this week: *"I think we've achieved AGI."*

The question is no longer *will AI be capable enough?* It's *what are you building on top of it, right now?*

If you're in recruiting, stop waiting for your ATS to catch up. Start building. If you're in sales or marketing, the teams already running AI are compounding their output every week. The gap is widening.

## 2. Mistral Small 4: One Open-Source Model for Everything

[Mistral announcement](https://mistral.ai/news/mistral-small-4)

Mistral released a single open-source model that handles instructions, reasoning, and multimodal tasks. Until now, you needed multiple models for a complete AI pipeline — and multiple API bills to match.

Mistral Small 4 collapses that into one. Self-host it and your per-call costs drop to zero. For small teams automating recruiting or ops workflows, this is a real budget unlock.

---

## 3. OpenAI Buys Astral: They Now Own Your Python Dev Tools

[OpenAI announcement](https://openai.com/index/openai-to-acquire-astral/)

OpenAI acquired Astral — the company behind `uv` and `ruff`, tools millions of developers use every day to manage Python packages and lint code. Both stay open source for now.

But think about what this means structurally: the company whose models you use now also controls the tools you use to build with them. That's a lot of leverage in one place. Worth thinking about before you go deeper on that stack.

---

## 4. Cursor Composer 2: A Fine-Tuned Open-Source Model Beats Claude Opus

[VentureBeat](https://venturebeat.com/technology/cursors-new-coding-model-composer-2-is-here-it-beats-claude-opus-4-6-but)

Cursor's new Composer 2 is built on a fine-tuned version of Kimi K2.5 — an open-source Chinese model — and it benchmarks better than Claude Opus 4.6 on coding tasks.

The frontier is no longer just OpenAI and Anthropic. Before you commit to an AI tool for your team, run a benchmark on your actual tasks. The winner might surprise you, and it might cost significantly less.

---

## 5. You're Probably Using AI Coding Agents Wrong

[Matt Shumer on X](https://x.com/mattshumer_/status/2035059149571297326)

A viral thread from HyperWrite's Matt Shumer — backed by OpenAI's Greg Brockman — makes a point that applies to every AI workflow, not just coding.

Most people start a fresh chat for every task. That's like hiring a great contractor and firing them every evening. The fix: keep one persistent thread per project or client. Let the agent accumulate context.

In sales, one thread per client with past emails, objections, and wins. In recruiting, one thread per role with the job spec and interview notes. The output quality compounds. Most people are starting from zero every time and wondering why results are mediocre.

---

## 6. Sahil Lavingia Turned His Book Into 9 AI Workflows

[Sahil on X](https://x.com/shl/status/2036162956761715096)

Gumroad founder Sahil Lavingia converted *The Minimalist Entrepreneur* into 9 Claude Code skills — `/validate-idea`, `/first-customers`, `/pricing`, and more. Each one operationalizes a chapter's advice as an interactive workflow.

This is the model: take what you already know, structure it, and turn it into something your team can run consistently — or something you can license to others in your industry. Your expertise is an asset that doesn't have to live only in your head.
