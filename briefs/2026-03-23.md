### 4. Yann LeCun on AI Safety: "Agency + No World Model = Danger"
- **Hook:** Yann LeCun posted a sharp take on AI safety that cuts against the mainstream narrative — and it's getting attention. His argument: the danger isn't AI agency itself, it's agency without the ability to predict consequences.
- **Why it matters:** LeCun argues the solution to unsafe AI agents isn't to limit agency, but to build agents with world models that can predict outcomes and apply safety guardrails before acting. He says he's been making this case for over five years. This is a direct counter to the "slow down agentic AI" school of thought, and it comes from one of the most credentialed voices in the field.
- **Implication:** For anyone building AI agents right now — whether for recruiting workflows, sales automation, or content pipelines — this framing is practically useful. The question isn't "should my agent have autonomy?" but "does my agent have a model of what will happen before it acts?" That's a design principle you can apply today.
- **Type:** Thought Leader
- **URL:** [x.com/ylecun/status/2034241159086645629](https://x.com/ylecun/status/2034241159086645629)

### 6. Dan Shipper: "How to Never Lose Your Job to AI — Just Surf the Models"
- **Hook:** Dan Shipper posted a framing that's worth reading twice: frontier models outclass humans at any knowledge that can be written down — but people who use frontier models in their field generate new, tacit, situational expertise that the models don't have yet.
- **Why it matters:** This is one of the cleaner articulations of the "human + AI" advantage that's been floating around. Shipper's point is that the edge isn't in knowing things — it's in generating new situational knowledge through the act of using AI in your specific context. The models can't replicate that because it hasn't been written down yet.
- **Implication:** For anyone in recruiting, marketing, or sales using AI tools: your competitive advantage isn't prompt engineering in the abstract — it's the domain-specific judgment you develop by running AI through your actual workflows, with your actual data, in your actual context. That's the moat. Build it deliberately.
