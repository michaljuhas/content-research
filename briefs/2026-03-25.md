### 6. A Supply Chain Attack Hit LiteLLM — One of the Most Popular AI Libraries
- **Hook:** Simon Willison flagged something alarming today: LiteLLM, one of the most widely used Python libraries for working with AI models, was hit by a supply chain attack. A compromised update was pushed to PyPI — the main Python package repository.
- **Why it matters:** LiteLLM is used by thousands of developers to route calls across OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, and other APIs. A malicious update in that library could affect any project that auto-updates dependencies. Willison confirmed the package has now been marked "quarantined" on PyPI, so new installs should be blocked — but anyone who pulled the bad version is potentially exposed.
- **Implication:** If you or your team uses LiteLLM in any AI project, check your dependency versions immediately. This is also a broader wake-up call: as AI tooling matures, it becomes a high-value target for supply chain attacks. Lock your dependency versions, audit your `requirements.txt`, and don't auto-update AI libraries in production without review.
- **Type:** Interesting
- **URL:** [Simon Willison on X — LiteLLM quarantined on PyPI](https://x.com/simonw/status/2036451896970584167)
